PROBLEM MATCHUPS — why you lose, in order

  1. Staraptor inversion (0-3). The bird is harmless unboosted (Ghold immunity); the Charm/FD partner is the whole team. Losses came from: Fake Out at the bird instead of the Prankster, Triple Axel miss, Incin fielded (its Intimidate feeds the mega, its body feeds CC). Execution checklist: partner dies T1, Ghold walls, no stat-drop moves ever.
  2. Dual weather (0-2). Their Weather Ball flips type with the weather: rain-mode kills Blaziken, sun-mode kills Ghold. Rule: count setters at preview — one Drizzle mon anywhere = rain rules. Kill Pelipper first, then it's a pure sun game you're 3-1 in.
  3. Zard+Gambit goodstuff (0-1). Conflict: the lure wants Incin+Tsareena, the sun wants Incin+Blaziken. Resolution: sun wins — lead the double Fire resist, bank Tsareena in back as the Gambit answer. Your Gambit/Ghold both stay home (Heat Wave 2x / sun WB 2x).
  4. EQ cores. Structural: only Whimsi and Tsareena are EQ-neutral; Blaziken/Incin/Ghold/Gambit all take 2x. No fix within the six — it's a positioning matchup forever. Kill the EQ user immediately; never 3+ EQ-weak in a bring.
  5. Mega Gengar (+Sableye). Mega Gengar OHKOs Ghold (Shadow Ball) and 2HKOs everything frail; Sableye Encores your Protects. Taunt Sableye with TSAREENA (non-Prankster Taunt works on Dark). Gengar dies to Sucker/Kowtow (2x) — it's a Gambit game.

STRUCTURAL TRUTHS OF THE SIX

  • Speed tier gap: Tsareena (144) is under the 167 club (Zard/Chomp/Staraptor base) — she cannot lead into them without Tailwind or a redirect. She is a MID-GAME mon vs fast teams.
  • Only two Fire resists (Incin, Blaziken) and one special wall (Ghold) — every sun/spread team probes exactly there.
  • No Wide Guard, no Fake Out immunity, no EQ immunity. Spread damage is answered by positioning only.
  • Gambit is your best mon under enemy TR and your worst vs enemy sun.
  • Ghold closes games (6 endgames won) but cannot be adjacent to: enemy Gambit, Grimmsnarl, Farigiraf (Foul Play), Knock Off users, Mega Gengar, sun Weather Ball.
  • The lure still wins games it never fires in: enemy Gambits stayed home in ~half of Gambit-team games = you played 4v5 at preview.
